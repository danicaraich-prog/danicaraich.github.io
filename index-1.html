Essays 

The Military-Industrial Complex 

This op-ed examines the enduring influence of the military-industrial complex on U.S. nuclear weapons policy. It argues that political and economic incentives embedded within the defense sector continue to shape modernization decisions and budget priorities. 

U.S. Diplomacy

This project supported the development of the Nuclear Diplomacy Crash Course, a video series produced by the American Academy of Diplomacy featuring senior diplomats and national security experts. The series explored the history of nuclear deterrence, arms control negotiations, and the diplomatic challenges posed by modern nuclear proliferation.

The Role of Liberal Education in Democracy

This paper examines the role of liberal education in sustaining democratic society through a comparative analysis of The Republic by Plato and Democracy in America by Alexis de Tocqueville. Using Plato’s allegory of the cave as a philosophical framework, the paper argues that education functions as a form of intellectual liberation, enabling individuals to question dominant narratives and pursue knowledge of the good.

Rohingya Accountability and International Justice

This paper analyzes international legal mechanisms for accountability in response to the persecution of the Rohingya population in Myanmar. It highlights both the possibilities and limitations of international law in responding to mass atrocities.

Gentrification and the Rise of the Creative Class

This research investigates the relationship between creative-class driven urban development and neighborhood change in Pittsburgh and Washington, D.C. It analyzes how economic redevelopment and shifting demographics contribute to displacement and uneven health outcomes among long-term residents.
