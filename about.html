About Me

I'm Danica Raich, a first-year law student at the University of Pittsburgh School of Law with a passion for international law and policy.

Before law school, I studied International Studies and Political Thought at American University in Washington, D.C., where I was a Lincoln Scholar and Army ROTC Cadet at Georgetown University. My undergraduate thesis explored nuclear weapons and the military industrial complex, which deepened my interest in how legal frameworks shape global security. 

During my time in D.C., I interned at the American Academy of Diplomacy, where I worked alongside former ambassadors and senior foreign policy leaders on diplomatic initiatives, including a video series on nuclear weapons and treaty negotiation. I also worked as a Junior Fellow at the McCain Institute, supporting the strategic communications team.

Outside of the classroom, I'm a member of the Allegheny County and Pennsylvania Bar Associations. I have intermediate proficiency in Russian and a lasting interest in how language and culture intersect with diplomacy.
